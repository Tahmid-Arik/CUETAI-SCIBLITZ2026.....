
DISEASE KNOWLEDGE BASE


Disease Name: Type 2 Diabetes
Identifying Characteristics: Excessive thirst, frequent urination, unexplained weight loss, constant fatigue, blurry vision.
Reasons: Insulin resistance, genetic predisposition, sedentary lifestyle, obesity, poor diet high in refined carbohydrates.
Treatment Advice: Consult an endocrinologist for personalized medication (like Metformin) or insulin therapy. Monitor blood glucose levels daily. Focus on low-glycemic foods and regular physical exercise.

Disease Name: Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)
Identifying Characteristics: Severe headaches, chest pain, dizziness, difficulty breathing, irregular heartbeat. (Often asymptomatic, known as a silent killer).
Reasons: Chronic stress, high sodium (salt) intake, lack of physical activity, smoking, alcohol consumption, genetics.
Treatment Advice: Regular blood pressure monitoring. Take prescribed antihypertensive medications consistently. Adopt the DASH diet (high in fruits, vegetables, and lean protein, low in salt). Engage in 30 minutes of moderate aerobic exercise daily.


GENERAL HEALTH & WELLNESS ADVICE (User-Demand Driven)
Focus Area: Good Health Maintenance & Prevention
1. Hydration: Drink at least 3-4 liters of filtered water daily to maintain cellular function.
2. Sleep Hygiene: Ensure 7-8 hours of uninterrupted sleep every night to facilitate tissue repair and cognitive reset.
3. Physical Activity: Aim for a minimum of 150 minutes of moderate exercise (brisk walking, cycling) per week.
4. Dietary Balance: Maintain a colorful plate comprising 50% vegetables/fruits, 25% lean proteins, and 25% complex carbohydrates. Reduce processed sugar intake to near zero.
5. Mental Wellness: Practice daily mindfulness or deep breathing exercises for 10 minutes to manage stress hormones like cortisol.
