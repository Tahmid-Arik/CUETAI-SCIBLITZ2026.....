## Data 01:- Common Cold (সর্দি-কাশি)
 * **Identifying Characteristics:** Gradual onset of a runny or stuffy nose, sneezing, a mild sore throat, a nagging cough, and watery eyes. A low-grade fever (100°F to 101°F or 37.8°C to 38.3°C) and mild body aches may be present, particularly in young children. Nasal discharge may start clear but naturally becomes thicker and yellow or green over a few days; this is a normal sign of the immune system working and does not automatically mean a bacterial infection has developed.
 * **Reasons:** An acute viral infection of the upper respiratory tract. While more than 200 different viruses can cause a cold, the **Rhinovirus** is the overwhelming culprit. The virus is highly contagious and spreads rapidly when an infected person coughs or sneezes, launching airborne respiratory droplets into the air. It also spreads through direct skin-to-skin contact or by touching contaminated everyday surfaces (like doorknobs or phones) and then touching your own eyes, nose, or mouth.
 * **Treatment Advice:** Because the common cold is viral, antibiotics are completely ineffective and should not be used. Treatment focuses entirely on easing symptoms while the body fights off the virus, which typically takes 7 to 10 days. Get plenty of rest and maximize your fluid intake. Over-the-counter (OTC) pain relievers like acetaminophen or ibuprofen can reduce fever and soothe a sore throat. Use saline nasal drops or sprays to loosen thick mucus, or use a cool-mist humidifier in the room to make breathing easier. Avoid giving OTC cough and cold medications to children under age 4 due to the risk of serious side effects.
 * **Local Action Protocol (Bangladesh):** For the Common Cold (সর্দি-কাশি) in Chattogram, isolate the patient as much as possible and practice strict handwashing to stop family transmission. Manage fever, body aches, and headaches safely using **Paracetamol**; avoid unnecessary OTC combination cold syrups without a doctor's guidance. Maintain hydration and soothe a raw throat cheaply using **Ginger-Honey Tea (আদা-মধু চা)**, warm **Saltwater Gargles (লবণ-গরম পানি)**, and fresh **Lemon-Zinc juice**, skipping costly imported immunity supplements. If the patient develops a persistent high fever (102°F+ or 38.9°C+ for more than 3 days), severe wheezing, difficulty breathing, an unyielding earache, or if a child refuses to feed and becomes unusually listless, seek immediate medical care at the outpatient or emergency wing of **Chittagong Medical College Hospital (CMCH)**, **Chittagong General Hospital (Anderkilla)**, or consult a doctor instantly via the 24/7 government health hotline at **16263**.

## Data 02:- Chickenpox (জলবসন্ত)
 * **Identifying Characteristics:** An intensely itchy skin rash that erupts into clusters of small, fluid-filled blisters resting on a red base (often described as "dewdrops on a rose petal"). The rash usually starts on the chest, back, or face before spreading across the entire body. It is accompanied by a fever, fatigue, a persistent headache, and a loss of appetite. The blisters eventually break open, ooze, and form crusty scabs.
 * **Reasons:** An acute respiratory infection caused by the highly contagious Varicella-zoster virus (VZV). The virus enters the body through the inhalation of airborne respiratory droplets or via direct contact with the fluid from the broken blisters of an infected person. Once contracted, it multiplies in the respiratory tract before spreading to the skin.
 * **Treatment Advice:** Treatment centers on soothing the intense itching and preventing secondary bacterial skin infections from scratching. Apply calamine lotion, give cool baths mixed with baking soda or colloidal oatmeal, and keep the child's fingernails trimmed short. Acetaminophen can be used to lower fever; strictly avoid ibuprofen and aspirin during chickenpox, as ibuprofen has been linked to severe, deep tissue skin infections, and aspirin can trigger Reye's syndrome. For individuals at high risk of complications, doctors may prescribe an antiviral medication like acyclovir if caught early. The definitive line of defense is prevention via the Varicella vaccine.
 * **Local Action Protocol (Bangladesh):** For Chickenpox (জলবসন্ত) in Chattogram, isolate the patient immediately and treat fever **only with Paracetamol**—strictly avoid Ibuprofen (linked to severe deep-tissue infections) and Aspirin (fatal Reye's syndrome risk). Soothe itching using calamine lotion, trimmed fingernails, and cool oatmeal or baking soda baths. Maintain hydration affordably with **Green Coconut Water (ডাবের পানি)**, **Lemon juice (লেবুর শরবত)**, and soft **Rice Gruel (জাউ ভাত)** instead of costly supplements. If the patient develops a persistent high fever, breathing difficulties, extreme lethargy, or if blisters become swollen, red, and filled with pus, seek immediate medical attention at **Chittagong Medical College Hospital (CMCH)**, **Chittagong General Hospital (Anderkilla)**, or call the 24/7 government health hotline at **16263**.
